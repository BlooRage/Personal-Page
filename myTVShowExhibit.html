Lights, Camera, Favorites! Jerome’s TV Series Picks!

     My favorite TV series revolve around themes of medicine, emergency response, gore, Violence, law, family, fantasy, adventure, politics, science, dragons, and democracy. If you’ve ever wondered why I watch so many medical dramas despite being an IT student, the reason is simple—I have always been fascinated by the medical field. As a child, I dreamed of becoming a pediatric doctor, drawn to the idea of helping and caring for young patients. However, due to personal reasons, I chose a different path and pursued IT instead. Despite this shift, my love for medical-related shows never faded. These series captivate me not only for their engaging storylines but also for the knowledge they impart, the ethical dilemmas they explore, and the life-or-death situations that test human resilience. Beyond medical dramas, I also enjoy anime and cartoons, which offer a different kind of storytelling rich in creativity, lessons, and unique perspectives on life. Each film or series I watch has something valuable to offer, whether it’s deep emotional connections, thought-provoking conflicts, or inspiring life lessons. No matter the genre, I appreciate stories that challenge the mind, evoke emotions, and leave a lasting impact. Grey's Anatomy

Air Date: March 27, 2005
Seasons: 21 Seasons
Genre: Medical | Drama | Melodrama
Rating:

         Watching this series has deepened my interest in the medical field. Initially, I wanted to be a pediatric doctor, but after exploring different specialties, I feel drawn to general surgery. I always enjoyed the meaningful quotes in each episode, but as the original cast members left one by one, the show gradually shifted its focus from medical cases to the personal lives of the remaining characters, which felt disappointing.

Suits

Air Date: October 04, 2017
Seasons: 9 Seasons
Genre: Legal Drama
Rating:

         Suits is one of the best TV series I have watched, offering intense courtroom battles and valuable insights into the legal world. The show brilliantly portrays the challenges faced by a law firm, with outstanding performances from the cast. Its perfectly executed ending and powerful quotes make it even more remarkable. The finale beautifully wraps up each character’s journey, leaving a satisfying and well-deserved conclusion for the series.

Modern Family

Air Date: September 23, 2009
Seasons: 11 Seasons
Genre: Mockumentary | Sitcom | Romance | Family
Rating:

         Modern Family is also one of the best TV series I have watched. It is a family documentary-style comedy that portrays modern family life in America. Although not entirely accurate, it delivers humor while offering valuable life lessons in business, personal growth, and decision-making. The show beautifully highlights the importance of emotions and staying connected with family. 911

Air Date: January 3, 2018
Seasons: 8 Seasons
Genre: Action | Drama | Thriller
Rating:

         After I finished 911 last year, a new season was released, and I just finished watching it in March 2025. I have always loved this series because it is informative, emotional, and intense. Black Clover

Air Date: October 03, 2017
Seasons: 4 Seasons
Genre: Fantasy | Adventure | Comedy
Rating:

         Black Clover felt really long when I first started watching it, but as I continued, the story became more interesting. In a world where magic exists, there is a boy who was born without any magical abilities. Despite this, he pushed himself to grow stronger. When the time came for everyone to receive their magic books, he did not get one at first. However, after an unexpected incident, he obtained a rare grimoire with anti-magic, the only one of its kind in their world. This unique ability set him on an incredible journey to prove himself.

Moriarty the Patriot

Air Date: October 11, 2020
Seasons: 2 Seasons
Genre: Crime | Mystery | Thriller
Rating:

         Moriarty the Patriot is a brilliant twist on the classic Sherlock Holmes story, told from Moriarty’s perspective. I love how it portrays him as an anti-hero fighting against corruption, making you question justice itself. The strategic mind games, intense rivalry with Sherlock, and stunning Victorian-era visuals kept me hooked. While some parts felt slow, the story’s depth and moral complexity made it a fascinating watch. The way each plan unfolds so perfectly makes every episode thrilling to watch. I would really recommend this film.
