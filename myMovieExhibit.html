Lights, Camera, Favorites! Jerome’s Must-Watch Movies!

     I love watching horror movies; I enjoy the jumpscares, the thrill, and the way they truly make me scared. But I also love musicals and animated films, especially those that focus on family and drama. Romantic and comedy movies? I’m all in—they give me different perspectives on love, relationships, and life. Every movie I watch leaves an impression on me, whether it’s through its story, emotions, or unforgettable moments. The Conjuring

Movie Premiere: July 19, 2013
Genre: Horror, Thriller, Supernatural
Rating:

         The Conjuring really scared me. This was one of the first horror movies I ever watched, and it still gives me chills. Bathsheba freaks me out with all the possessions and jumpscares. The production in this movie is seriously good, from the sound effects to the eerie atmosphere—it all adds up to an unforgettable horror experience.

The Conjuring 2

Movie Premiere: June 10, 2016
Genre: Horror, Thriller, Supernatural
Rating:

         Each of The Conjuring films introduces a new horror character, but one of the scariest for me is The Conjuring 2, featuring the terrifying villain, Valak. The scene that truly scared me was when Valak appeared as a shadow, slowly creeping towards a picture frame, only to suddenly attack Lorraine. The eerie atmosphere, combined with the intense suspense, made it one of the most unforgettable horror moments.

Insidious

Movie Premiere: April 1, 2011
Genre: Horror, Thriller, Supernatural
Rating:

         Well, Insidious made it to my list because it is one of the scariest movies I have ever watched. It still gives me nightmares, goosebumps, and even the creeps of being alone in the dark. The eerie atmosphere, chilling soundtrack, and terrifying visuals make it an unforgettable horror experience. The concept of astral projection and the Further adds a unique and disturbing twist to the film, making it stand out from other horror movies. The suspenseful build-up and unexpected jump scares make every scene more intense, leaving a lasting impression to me or on any horror fan.

Annabelle The Creation

Movie Premiere: August 11, 2017
Genre: Horror, Mystery, Thriller
Rating:

         Out of all the Annabelle movies, from The Conjuring to Annabelle Comes Home, my favorite is Annabelle: Creation. This film masterfully portrays how the curse of Annabelle originated, giving us a chilling backstory filled with eerie moments and disturbing twists. The suspense, atmosphere, and terrifying sequences kept me on edge, making it one of the best horror prequels I've ever seen. The way it connects to the rest of the Conjuring universe is both impressive and haunting. The film's ability to build tension while delivering solid scares makes it an unforgettable experience for any horror fan.

Fear Street Movie Series

Movie Premiere: July 2, 2021 (Part One: 1994)
Genre: Horror, Mystery, Thriller
Rating:

         The Fear Street movie series is a must-watch for me every Halloween! It is one of the greatest horror films I have seen, and I love how they managed to connect three different movies into one gripping story. The blend of mystery, supernatural elements, and slasher horror kept me hooked from start to finish. The way they explored different time periods while maintaining a thrilling and cohesive narrative was truly impressive. The eerie atmosphere, intense kills, and unexpected twists made it an unforgettable experience that I highly recommend to any horror fan. Each movie stands strong on its own, but together they made it more scary.

IT Chapter 1

Movie Premiere: September 8, 2017
Genre: Horror, Thriller
Rating:

         When IT Chapter 1 was released, I watched it, and for me, it was one of the most terrifying movies that truly scared my soul. From Georgie going missing to the eerie and unsettling moments with Pennywise, the film kept me on edge the entire time. The way it combined psychological horror with intense jump scares made it an unforgettable experience. The friendships within the Losers' Club added depth to the story, making the horror feel even more real and personal. The cinematography and sound design were incredibly effective in building suspense, making every encounter with Pennywise even more chilling.

50 First Dates

Movie Premiere: February 13, 2004
Genre: Romantic Comedy, Drama
Rating:

         When Henry said to Lucy “You erased me from your memories because you thought you were holding me back from having a full and happy life. But you made a mistake. Being with you is the only way I could have a full and happy life. You’re the girl of my dreams—and apparently, I’m the man of yours.” This line made me think that I could erase all the people in the world and still choose you. This movie shows love, commitment, and sacrifice, proving that love is about effort and choosing someone every day. It reminds us that love isn’t always easy, but it’s worth it. Sometimes, the best kind of love is the one that makes you fight for it over and over again.

How to Train Your Dragon Movies

Movie Premiere: March 26, 2010 (First Film)
Genre: Animation, Adventure, Fantasy
Rating:

         One of my favorite cartoons to watch during junior high school was the How to Train Your Dragon movie series, including the TV series. My love for this franchise is never-ending, and I’m always willing to rewatch these movies. The adventures, challenges, and discoveries in a world filled with dragons create an exciting and emotional journey. Hiccup and Toothless’ bond teaches valuable lessons about trust, friendship, and perseverance. Each film builds on the last, showcasing Hiccup’s growth from an outcast to a true leader. The stunning animation, thrilling action, and heartfelt moments make this series unforgettable. I highly recommend it to anyone who loves fantasy and adventure.

Coco

Movie Premiere: November 22, 2017
Genre: Animation, Adventure, Family
Rating:

         I love the music in Coco and how it beautifully portrays Miguel’s passion for music despite his family’s disapproval. The film takes us on an emotional journey through the Land of the Dead, blending stunning visuals, heartfelt storytelling, and a deep connection to family and tradition. Miguel’s relationship with his great-grandmother Coco is touching, reminding us of the importance of remembering and cherishing our loved ones. The soundtrack, especially Remember Me, adds to the emotional weight of the story, highlighting how music connects generations. Watching Coco made me reflect on my own family and the significance of honoring our roots. With its moving themes of love, dreams, and legacy, Coco is an unforgettable film that I highly recommend.

How to Make Millions Before Grandma Dies

Movie Premiere: April 4, 2024
Genre: Drama, Family
Rating:

         Watching this movie twice gave me a deep appreciation for its message. The first time, I watched it with my girlfriend, thinking I was strong enough to handle it, but I wasn’t. I cried a lot, reflecting on the sacrifices my parents have made and what the future holds if I ever face a similar situation. It made me realize how precious time with family truly is. The lessons and values in this film struck me so deeply that I watched it again with my siblings. To my surprise, they understood the story despite the language difference, proving how universal its message is. This movie is a must-watch as it teaches the importance of cherishing our parents while they are still here, strong, happy, and with us. I highly recommend it to everyone; its life lessons will truly shift your heart.

10 Things I Hate About You

Movie Premiere: March 31, 1999
Genre: Romantic Comedy, Teen Drama
Rating:

         Watching 10 Things I Hate About You with my girlfriend opened my eyes to the possibilities of love and relationships. The film blends humor, romance, and teenage rebellion, following the witty Kat Stratford and the charming Patrick Verona as they navigate high school romance. With its iconic 90s vibes, clever dialogue, and heartfelt moments, it left a lasting impression on me. Beyond the love story, it explores themes of independence and self-expression, making it more than just a typical rom-com. I highly recommend it to anyone who enjoys nostalgic, meaningful, and witty romantic films.

X Trilogy (Pearl, X, Maxxxine)

Movie Premiere: Pearl (2022), X (2022), Maxxxine (2024)
Genre: Horror, Slasher, Psychological Thriller
Rating:

         The X Trilogy is one of the most unique horror movies I have watched, portraying obsession, fame, madness, and psychological struggles. Pearl (2022) follows a young woman’s descent into insanity as she dreams of fame. X (2022) is a brutal slasher where filmmakers making an adult film are hunted by a deranged elderly couple. Maxxxine (2024) follows Maxine Minx in 1980s Hollywood, fighting for stardom while her past haunts her. I recommend this movie for those who are not innocent, as it contains sexual scenes and intense horror elements.
