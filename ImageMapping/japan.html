Welcome to Japan!

          I want to visit Japan because its rich culture, history, and modern innovations fascinate me. Inspired by anime, I became curious about traditional Japanese houses, authentic cuisine, and the unique blend of old and new in their streets. From serene temples to vibrant cityscapes, I want to experience Japan’s beauty firsthand, explore its traditions, and immerse myself in the culture that has captivated me for so long.

TRAVEL

         If you’re planning to travel to Japan from Manila, it’s pretty easy! You can take a direct flight from NAIA to Tokyo, Osaka, or Nagoya, which takes around four to five hours. Airlines like Philippine Airlines, Japan Airlines, ANA, Cebu Pacific, and Jetstar have flights. Just make sure to secure a Japan visa with all the required documents before your trip. Once you arrive, getting around is a breeze with Japan’s efficient trains and buses. It’s going to be an exciting adventure!

LANDMARKS

There are so many amazing places to visit in Japan, but personally, I’m most excited about exploring its busy streets and vibrant city life. Imagine standing in front of the iconic Tokyo Tower, taking in the breathtaking city view, especially at night when the skyline lights up. It’s the perfect spot for sightseeing and capturing stunning photos. Then there’s Shibuya Crossing, the world’s busiest pedestrian crossing—just experiencing the energy of the crowd feels like stepping into a scene straight out of an anime or a movie. I’d love to explore the nearby shops, try some local street food, and maybe even visit the famous Hachiko statue. And of course, Osaka is a must-visit. It’s known for its lively atmosphere, especially in places like Dotonbori, where neon lights reflect off the canal and the streets are filled with mouthwatering street food like takoyaki and okonomiyaki. I’d definitely stop by Universal Studios Japan for the anime and movie-themed attractions or explore Osaka Castle to get a glimpse of Japan’s rich history. Japan has so much to offer, from cultural landmarks to modern cityscapes, but for me, the thrill of being in the heart of the action amidst the crowds, lights, and energy is something I can’t wait to experience.

EXPERIENCE

There are so many things to experience in Japan, but what I really want to do is eat authentic ramen, drink real matcha, and wear a kimono. Imagine sitting in a cozy ramen shop, slurping a hot, flavorful bowl of noodles—it’s a must-try! I also want to taste authentic matcha in a traditional tea house and enjoy its rich, earthy flavor. And of course, I would love to wear a kimono and walk through Kyoto’s historic streets, fully embracing Japan’s culture. These are the experiences I’m most excited about!
