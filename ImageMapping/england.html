Welcome to England!

          England has always fascinated me with its mix of history, culture, and modern city life. From its iconic landmarks to its cozy countryside, there’s so much to explore. I want to walk through the streets of London, see the majestic castles, and experience the charm of English traditions. Whether it’s visiting historic sites, enjoying a proper English breakfast, or just strolling through a quiet village, I know England has a lot to offer.

TRAVEL

         Traveling to England from Manila is an adventure in itself. The quickest way is to fly to London, usually landing at Heathrow Airport. Since there are no direct flights, I’d likely have a stopover in Dubai, Doha, or Singapore before reaching my destination. Once in London, getting around is hassle-free with its efficient trains and buses, making it easy to explore the country’s rich history and vibrant cities.

LANDMARKS

         The things I want to do in England include seeing the London Bridge, taking in the breathtaking view of the city while walking across it. I also want to visit Big Ben, one of the most iconic landmarks in London, and hear its famous chimes. Another must-see for me is the Roman Baths in Bath, where I can step back in time and experience the ancient history of England. Exploring these places would make my trip unforgettable!

EXPERIENCE

One of the most famous is Glastonbury Festival, a massive music and arts festival where top artists perform, and the atmosphere is electric. If I want something more traditional, Bonfire Night (Guy Fawkes Night) is a must-see, with fireworks lighting up the sky every November 5th to commemorate the failed Gunpowder Plot.

Of course, I can’t leave England without having a full English breakfast, a hearty meal with eggs, bacon, sausages, baked beans, toast, and more! And as a fan of magic and fantasy, the Harry Potter Studio Tour is definitely on my list—to see the sets, props, and behind-the-scenes secrets that brought the Wizarding World to life.
